The Gospel Music Boom in Latin America & The US

An in-depth look at the "Silent Giant" of the music industry. From the mega-churches of Brazil to the bilingual worship movements in the US, Gospel music is outpacing secular genres in engagement and loyalty.

The "Hallel" Effect

The Latin American Gospel market is unique. It operates largely outside the traditional major label ecosystem, driven by church networks and independent distribution. However, recent years have seen a massive shift. With Brazil standing as the second-largest Christian market globally and the US Latin population bridging languages, the total addressable market is estimated at over 125 million highly engaged listeners.

The Digital Sanctuary

The pandemic (2020) accelerated digital adoption in churches, leading to a massive spike in streaming.

Unlike secular music, YouTube retains a disproportionately high market share (approx 55%) versus audio-only platforms like Spotify. Gospel consumers "watch" worship services and lyric videos religiously.

Trend Alert:
The "Collaborative Release" strategy (e.g., Maverick City + Elevation) has doubled cross-border streams since 2021.
